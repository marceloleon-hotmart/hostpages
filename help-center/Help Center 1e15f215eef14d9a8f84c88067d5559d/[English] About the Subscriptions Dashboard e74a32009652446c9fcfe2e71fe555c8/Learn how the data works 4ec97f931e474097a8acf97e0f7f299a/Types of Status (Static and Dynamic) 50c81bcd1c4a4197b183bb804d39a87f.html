Types of Status (Static and Dynamic)

When we talk about subscriptions, it's essential to understand that they have two types of status: static and dynamic. The static status shows the current situation of the subscription, while the dynamic status shows changes over time. This duality provides a comprehensive view of the state and evolution of subscriptions.

Static Status

Starded

Occurs when the sale is initiated. If the payment is successfully completed, the subscription is confirmed and becomes active; otherwise, it remains inactive.

Active

Indicates an active subscription, in the current period, with the last recurrence payment confirmed, and the buyer has access to the content. The status is independent of payment for previous recurrences before the last one and may include subscriptions still in the trial period.

Inactive

Occurs when the activation of an initiated subscription fails. The first recurrence was not paid and is overdue.

Note: It may happen that the same buyer generates a new subscription, pays, and activates it. In this case, they will generate a new subscription ID, while the inactive one remains registered.

Delayed

Active subscriptions with delayed payment for the last recurrence. They can be in one of three situations:

  • Automatic recovery attempt ended with no payment.
  • In Automatic recovery attempt by Hotmart.
  • Payment not approved.

Canceled

There are three types:

  • Canceled by the buyer: Subscription canceled by the buyer.
  • Canceled by the seller: An active subscription can be changed to this status if the producer decides to cancel it for some administrative reason.
  • Canceled by the administrator: Subscription canceled by the Hotmart Support Team when any irregularity is identified.

Expired

A subscription whose duration period has ended, common in cases of subscriptions with a maximum number of installments or single payment.

Notes: The recurrences charged during this period may all be settled or there may be some overdue. After this period, it is not possible to reactivate the same Subscription ID; if the Subscriber wants to renew, a new ID will be created.

You can check if there are overdue payments through the "List of unpaid recurrences" column and schedule new collection retries through the platform.

Dynamic Status

Initiated

This status is assigned to subscriptions when the sale is initiated, before enrollment. As long as the first payment is neither approved nor declined, the subscription remains with the dynamic status initiated.

New

When a subscription that did not exist or was in the initiated status becomes active, it receives the dynamic status new, indicating that it is a new active subscription.

Renewal

This dynamic status is assigned to subscriptions that were already in the active status in the previous period and remain in that status until the last update. We consider retention all subscriptions that remain active.

New Loss

These are subscriptions that were in the active status in the last period and, in the last update, are in any of the three canceled statuses. We consider a new loss all active subscriptions that became canceled between two periods.

New Late

These are subscriptions that were in the active status in the last period and, in the last update, have a pending payment, with the late status.

New Expired

These are subscriptions that were in the active or late status in the last period and, in the last update, are in the expired status. We consider a loss due to the end of the subscription period all active or late subscriptions that became finished between two periods.

Old Loss

These are subscriptions that were in some canceled or late status in the last period and, in the last update, are in any of the three canceled statuses. This status of old losses aims to list possible recoveries without affecting the subscription variation numbers between the two periods.

Quick Loss

These are subscriptions that were in the inactive or expired status and became canceled or late.

Old Late

These are subscriptions that were in some canceled or late status in the last period and, in the last update, are in the late status. This status of old late aims to list possible recoveries without affecting the subscription variation numbers between the two periods.

Reactivated

When an inactive subscription (canceled, late, or finished) becomes active, it receives the dynamic status reactivated, indicating the reactivation of an old subscription.

We also consider as reactivation old subscriptions that return with another product or offer.
